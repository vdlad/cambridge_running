**Fens** ![Stone bridge on the side of the trail on a shady day - Photo from Greatruns.com, photo links](http://greatruns.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/07/emerald-necklace-bike-and-running-paths-CP.png) # Overview The Emerald Necklace is a true gem. Fens are a type of wetland so there is plenty of swans, ducks, and geese to view. Some landmarks are the Boston Museam of Fine Art (MFA), the James P. Kelleher Rose Garden, and Jamacia Pond. Everything is pretty flat out here and there is a bit of concrete, but there are plenty of oppurtunites for crushed gravel trail if you know where to look. The prospects for bathrooms are notoriously less abundant than you think. Be warned, some of these trails do not get cleared in the winter so they get a little sloppy. Also be warned that some places get really dark. It is possible to do year round, but definetly more plesant in the light. The naming convention is driven by the main body of water: Jamacia Pond and the neighborhood known as the Back Bay Fens.
