**Harvard River Loops** ![The Charles up by Harvard on a frosty morning - Photo from Harvard's Twitter, photo links](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/DxmWqINWsAA2y_B.jpg) # Overview Some say this is the best part of the Esplanade. Others say this is the only place you need to run in Boston. It is a great spot that takes you deep in to Cambridge, past Harvard, all along the Charles. On a warm day there will be many pedestrians and cyclists. Over the summer, sections of memorial will be closed to cars to provide an even better experience. Since it is around the river, everything is very flat. The main surface is asphalt shared use path. There is a decent amount of soft surface in the form of single track on the side of the path and some crushed gravel sections. The esplande gets plowed decently fast and is well illuminated so it is a solid run year round. The Boston/Allston side of the river is a little narrower and unpleasent between Western and BU. The naming convention is driven by the bridges you use to cross the river. Forward is defined as staying on the Cambridge side.
